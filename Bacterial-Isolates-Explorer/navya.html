Bacterial Isolates Explorer: Your Guide to Bacterial Species and Antibiotic Susceptibility

Welcome to the Bacterial Isolates Explorer webpage! Here, you will find a comprehensive list of bacterial species, their strain number, product, origin, isolation method, source, and antibiotic susceptibility. Our database contains information on 8 different types of bacteria, including Lactobacillus acidophilus, Bifidobacterium bifidum, Lactococcus lactis, Streptococcus thermophilus, Lactobacillus reuteri, Lactobacillus rhamnosus, Lactobacillus plantarum, and Bifidobacterium infantis.

Each entry in our database contains details about the strain's isolation, source, and antibiotic susceptibility. For example, you can find information on the susceptibility of Lactobacillus acidophilus strain ATCC 314 to Ampicillin and Streptomycin. This information can be helpful in determining which antibiotics are most effective in treating infections caused by these bacterial strains.

We hope that this information will be useful to researchers, healthcare professionals, and anyone interested in learning more about bacterial species and their properties. Feel free to browse our database and explore the fascinating world of bacterial isolates!
